Brunswick County ARES Emergency Operations

The Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES) consists of licensed amateurs who have voluntarily registered their qualifications and equipment for communications duty at the local and county levels when disaster strikes.

Every licensed amateur, regardless of membership in the ARRL or any other organization, is eligible for membership. The only qualification is a sincere desire to serve our local community.

📂 Operational Documentation & Forms

Our local disaster protocols serve as an essential entry-level training resource. This framework is maintained by our planning committee, originally developed under the guidance of former Emergency Coordinator Ward Newcomb (N4PEX).

🎓 Training & Certification Requirements

All active tactical deployments must comply with the National Incident Management System (NIMS) and operate strictly under the Incident Command System (ICS) framework when officially mobilized.

The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) requires all field volunteers to be certified in the following core independent-study courses:

  • 🔟 IS-100 — Introduction to the Incident Command System
  • 🔠 IS-200 — Basic Incident Command System for Initial Response
  • 🔀 IS-700 — National Incident Management System (NIMS), An Introduction
  • 🌐 IS-800 — National Response Framework, An Introduction

⚠️ EOC Tactical Deployment Notice: Operators expected to deploy directly to Emergency Operations Centers (EOCs), mobile command posts, or public safety dispatch hubs (911 centers) where direct interfacing with public safety officials occurs must complete all four foundational FEMA modules.

These independent-study courses are internet-based, self-paced, and available free of charge from the FEMA Independent Study Course Directory .

🏡 Non-Certified Support Positions

You do not have to complete NIMS coursework to be an active asset in the club’s emergency network. Non-certified operators are highly valued and can attend planning meetings, serve as neighborhood storm spotters, participate in training exercises, and assist with shore station preparations.
